谈判技巧
Ella (Seller)
Good morning, Mr. Von Klaus, thank you for your patience; I’m calling to formally present our offer for the custom packaging units you inquired about, which I've just sent to your email.
早上好，冯·克劳斯先生，感谢您的耐心等待；我这次来电是就您之前咨询的定制包装单元向您正式报盘，报价单我已经发送到您的邮箱了。
Von Klaus (Buyer)
I've glanced at it, Ella, and quite frankly, I'm appalled by the audacity of these numbers; you must think my company prints money, because this "offer" is nothing short of daylight robbery compared to your competitors.
我大概扫了一眼，艾拉，坦白说，我被这些数字的厚颜无耻震惊了；你们一定觉得我公司是印钞的吧，因为跟你们的竞争对手相比，这个所谓的“报盘”简直就是光天化日下的抢劫。
Ella (Seller)
I completely understand your concern regarding the budget, but I must respectfully point out that comparing our premium, eco-friendly materials with the substandard options other suppliers likely quoted you is an unfair comparison that ignores the long-term brand value we guarantee.
我完全理解您对预算的顾虑，但我必须恭敬地指出，将我们优质、环保的材料与其他供应商可能给您报的低劣选项相提并论是不公平的，这种做法忽视了我们能保证的长期品牌价值。
Von Klaus (Buyer)
Spare me the marketing spiel about "brand value," because at this price point, I'm not buying value, I'm buying overpriced cardboard, so unless you can slash this total by at least fifteen percent right now, don't bother following up.
别跟我扯什么“品牌价值”的营销废话了，因为在这个价位上，我买的不是价值，是漫天要价的纸板，所以除非你现在能把总价至少砍掉15%，否则就别费心再跟进这件事了。
Ella (Seller)
While a fifteen percent reduction is mathematically impossible without compromising the structural integrity of the product, I am authorized to offer a five percent discount on the condition that you increase your initial order volume by twenty percent and agree to a Letter of Credit payment term.
虽然在不牺牲产品结构完整性的前提下，降价15%在数学上是不可能的，但我有权提供一个5%的折扣，条件是您能将首次订单量增加20%，并同意采用信用证的付款方式。
